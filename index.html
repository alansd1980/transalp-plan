Phase 3 · Nov – Jan 2027
Endurance Block
CAMP
▼
Build deep aerobic base through winter. This is where Transalp is actually won or lost. Embrace the dark months.
Cycling
TR Base/Build hybrid. More indoor sessions as daylight drops but outdoor rides protected for weekends. Volume is the priority — not intensity.
Strength
Maintain all strength gains. Do not let gym drop below 2 sessions per week through winter. This is where muscle is preserved heading into race prep.
🏕️ Home Camp — December or January
3-day back-to-back block. Day 1: 4hr ride. Day 2: 4hr ride. Day 3: 2.5hr recovery ride. Take half days off work. This replicates multi-day Alpine fatigue better than anything else. The body learns to keep going on tired legs — you cannot simulate this any other way.
Key Notes
  • FTP target: 270–280W by January
  • Christmas — maintain movement, don't panic about missed sessions
  • Alcohol awareness — you know the multi-day training effect
  • Weight target: 83–84kg by January

Key Principles
  • Protein first at every meal — not an afterthought
  • Batch boil 4–6 eggs on Sunday — removes friction all week
  • Fruit is good but doesn't move the protein needle
  • Honey is just sugar with a health halo — not needed
  • Alcohol — you already know the multi-day training effect
  • Gradual weight loss: 87kg → 80kg over 13 months — no crash dieting
  • Fuel properly on training days — don't restrict calories before hard sessions
MyFitnessPal Strategy
Don't log obsessively forever. Log consistently for 2–3 weeks to build awareness of where the gaps actually are. Then operate intuitively and dip back in for a reality check week monthly. Don't log exercise calories in MFP — TrainerRoad handles that and MFP overestimates cycling burn significantly.
